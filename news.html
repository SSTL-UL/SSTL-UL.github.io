Conference June 2026 Québec, Canada

Eight Research Contributions Shaping the Future of Mobility at CSCE 2026

The SSTL–LTID Lab participated in the 2026 Annual Conference of the Canadian Society for Civil Engineering / La Société canadienne de génie civil, held in Québec City. This major national event brought together researchers, professors, students, professionals, and industry partners to discuss current and future challenges in civil engineering.

During the conference, our laboratory presented eight scientific communications related to transportation engineering, travel behavior analysis, data science, and artificial intelligence. These contributions reflect the lab’s ongoing work on developing innovative, evidence-based solutions to support sustainable, accessible, resilient, and intelligent mobility systems.

Our research is closely connected to the current and future transportation challenges of Québec City. By combining advanced analytical methods with real-world mobility issues, SSTL–LTID aims to support better transportation planning, decision-making, and policy development.

The conference also provided an excellent opportunity to exchange ideas with the broader transportation community, strengthen collaborations, and highlight the work of our students, researchers, collaborators, and partners.

We sincerely thank all members of the organizing committee, the Transportation Specialty team, and everyone who contributed to the success of this event. We also congratulate our students and collaborators for their dedication and valuable contributions to the lab’s research activities.

Conference May 2026 Halifax, Canada

Participation in the 61st Annual Canadian Transportation Research Forum (CTRF)

The Smart and Sustainable Transportation Lab (SSTL) participated in the 61st Annual Canadian Transportation Research Forum (CTRF) Conference, held in Halifax, Nova Scotia, from May 24–27, 2026. CTRF is one of Canada's leading forums for transportation research and policy, bringing together academics, industry professionals, government representatives, and transportation practitioners to discuss emerging challenges and innovative solutions in the field of mobility.

During the conference, SSTL contributed through active participation in technical sessions, networking activities, and the renowned Bison Debate, a long-standing CTRF tradition that has been an integral part of the conference since 1996. This unique event encourages critical thinking and engaging discussions on key transportation issues facing Canada.

A highlight of this year's conference was the participation of our team member in the Bison Debate alongside Professor Martin Trépanier. Following a stimulating exchange of ideas and perspectives, their team was awarded the debate victory, bringing the prestigious Bison statue to Université Laval. This achievement represents a memorable moment for our laboratory and reflects our commitment to excellence in transportation research, communication, and knowledge exchange.

Beyond this accomplishment, the conference provided valuable opportunities to engage with researchers and practitioners from across Canada, strengthen existing collaborations, and explore new avenues for future research in sustainable and intelligent transportation systems.

Conference October 2025 Québec, Canada

Presentations at the Transportation Association of Canada (TAC)

SSTL participated in the 2025 Transportation Association of Canada (TAC) Annual Conference, the largest national gathering of transportation professionals in Canada. The conference brings together experts from industry, government (municipal, provincial, territorial, and federal), universities, and professional associations, offering a rich program of technical sessions, panels, workshops, and an exhibition showcasing the latest innovations in transportation.

During TAC 2025, our team contributed through multiple presentations highlighting ongoing research projects, as well as participation in panel sessions and poster sessions. This event provided an excellent opportunity to share our work, exchange ideas with the broader transportation community, and strengthen connections with partners working on real-world mobility challenges.

Lab life September 2025

SSTL Visual Identity — Logo Creation

To build a strong and consistent visual identity for SSTL, we organized an internal design competition to select the lab’s official logo and color palette. Team members reviewed multiple proposals and voted for the combination that best represents our mission in smart and sustainable transportation.

Following the selection, we applied the new identity across our communication materials and produced branded merchandise—including SSTL t-shirts and caps—to support outreach activities, conference participation, and lab visibility. This initiative helped strengthen our team culture while ensuring a professional and recognizable presence at events.
