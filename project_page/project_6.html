Carrots and sticks: assessing intervention effectiveness for sustainable mobility systems to reduce GHG emissions

Objective

This research focuses on bridging the gap between traditional transportation network theory and modern data-driven AI techniques. The goal is to create hybrid models that leverage the strengths of both approaches to optimize the performance, resilience, and sustainability of complex multimodal transportation systems.

Methodology

We are developing novel algorithms that integrate classical network equilibrium and traffic flow theories with deep reinforcement learning and predictive analytics. By using large-scale datasets from traffic sensors, GPS, and public transit APIs, our models can learn complex network dynamics and proactively manage congestion, incidents, and demand fluctuations. The research involves both simulation-based experiments and real-world case studies in collaboration with municipal transport authorities.

Expected Outcomes

This project will produce a new generation of decision support tools for transportation planners and operators. These tools will enable dynamic and adaptive management of urban mobility, including real-time traffic signal control, optimized transit scheduling, and proactive incident response strategies. Ultimately, our work aims to enhance urban mobility, reduce travel times, and lower the environmental impact of transportation.
