AI-Driven Demand Prediction for Electric Bike-Sharing System Planning

Objective

Traditional demand modeling for electric bike-sharing service often relies heavily on a station’s past demand to predict its future demand. As such, these models are well suited when forecasting for existing bike-sharing stations but struggle when it comes to new stations. This project aims to develop a new deep learning model which can accurately predict monthly demand for both existing and new stations of a bike-sharing system. 

Methodology

Our model integrates three aspects: spatial models such as graph neural networks, which exploit the relationships existing between the stations in the network; temporal models which focus on the historical demand sequence to obtain the prediction; spatiotemporal models which combine the two previous types to capture both spatial and temporal dependencies present in the data. These models help identify which structures are better for demand forecasting for the bicycle stations. 

The proposed model also integrates exogenous features to help fill the gap left by the lack of historical demand for new stations. We use built environment features, such as point-of-interest richness and density, to characterize the environment around each electric bike-sharing station. This allows us to see which features have an impact on bike-sharing demand. 

Outcomes and Impacts

Our research shows that combining the right spatial and temporal models, while adding exogenous features, can help obtain better predictions for new stations without having a negative impact on existing station predictions. Accurate predictions for new stations can then help plan where and how a bike-sharing system should be expanded to meet the needs of its users.
