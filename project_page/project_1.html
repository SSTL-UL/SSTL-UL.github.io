Machine Learning for New Approaches to Urban Mobility Simulation

Objective

Traditional urban mobility simulations are often limited by computational complexity and reliance on simplified behavioral assumptions. This project seeks to overcome these limitations by developing novel simulation methodologies powered by machine learning. Our goal is to create highly scalable, data-driven, and behaviorally realistic simulations that can accurately model the complexities of modern urban transportation.

Methodology

We are exploring the use of agent-based modeling combined with deep learning to simulate the decisions of millions of individual travelers. By training neural networks on large-scale mobility datasets, our simulated agents can learn and replicate complex human travel patterns. This allows us to test the impact of new technologies, policies, and infrastructure projects—such as autonomous vehicle fleets or congestion pricing—in a high-fidelity virtual environment before real-world implementation.

Expected Outcomes

This research will deliver a next-generation urban mobility simulation platform. This platform will serve as a powerful "digital twin" for cities, enabling urban planners to experiment with innovative transportation solutions in a risk-free setting. The insights gained from these simulations will lead to more efficient, equitable, and sustainable urban mobility planning and policy-making.
