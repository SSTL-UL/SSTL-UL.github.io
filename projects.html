The potential of battery electric trucks in forest transportation

This PhD thesis focuses on the development of optimization models in forest transportation. We will develop a heterogenous electric vehicle routing problems for the application of lumber and log hauling in forestry. The potential of electric trucks in forestry is evaluated in three stages: Strategic, tactical and operational. Each stage corresponds to one research paper. The strategic stage looks at the scale at which the technology has been deployed and its impact on the industry. The tactical plan looks into determining the optimal locations of installing charging stations. Operational plan seeks the best routes to choose amongst a given set of candidates in the network. For the tactical and operational plans, we develop optimization models based on a comprehensive energy consumption analysis of the network. This project will be executed in coordination with the FORAC industrial partners

Machine Learning for New Approaches to Urban Mobility Simulation

This project develops a transportation simulator that represents cars and roads as a network of connected points. By using artificial intelligence, it aims to capture how drivers adapt their routes based on traffic conditions and congestion levels. The focus is on making these simulations more data-driven and realistic, providing deeper insights into how transportation systems work and offering a safe virtual space to test new ideas for improving mobility.

Carrots and sticks: assessing intervention effectiveness for sustainable mobility systems to reduce GHG emissions

To achieve the goal of reducing emissions from 2005 levels by 40% in 2030, the government of Canada provides a number of measures to encourage people to change their travel modes to sustainable mobilities. Some measures are in favor of promoting alternative transport (e.g.,"carrots"), and others aim to discourage unsustainable modes (e.g., “sticks”). The objective of this research program is to develop new survey and experiment methods to get data, then to simulate and evaluate the effects of modal shift strategies on mobility, and finally to calculate the contribution of different types of strategies to GHG reduction (Carrots, Sticks, or Combined).
